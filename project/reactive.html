Development of a reactive suspension for vehicles

(TL;DR)

”(...) our purpose in designing the suspension and steering is to attempt to maintain the wheels at the optimum angle to the road surface at all times and under all conditions... This is the Holy Grail of the designer and is, in practice, unattainable”.

T. Pashley

This thesis emerges to develop a reactive suspension system that counterbalances the effects of lateral forces using conventional suspension elements without consuming any energy. In order to do so, a vehicle was acquired to conduct experimental tests on the conventional suspension setup and compare the data with the simulation model. Multibody software were used to analyze the vehicle’s behavior and develop new suspension concepts.

TL;DR

objectives

  • Provide an overview of suspension design and methodologies
  • Benchmark available solutions
  • Develop and analyze different concepts and select one to implement in the acquired vehicle
  • Validate the simulation model with experimental data
  • Develop the vehicle concept

TECHNICAL DETAILS

  • CAD - SolidWorks
  • FEA - SolidWorks and Hypermesh
  • Simulation - Adams and Matlab Simulink
  • Product Design and Process Planning

RESULTS

  • The proof of concept was demonstrated, in the sense that the developed concept achieved negative roll angles in a tilt table test
  • It was acknowledged that the springs’ anchor points should be located above the sprung mass CG to attain a negative body roll
  • For the concept developed it was determined that although it counters the body roll, it worsens the rollover safety
